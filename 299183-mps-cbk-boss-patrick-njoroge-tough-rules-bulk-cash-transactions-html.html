MPs want CBK boss Patrick Njoroge out over tough rules on bulk cash transactions

 Posted on April 1, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  528 words  |  Hettie Henneman

- The lawmakers protested regulations limiting daily cash transactions claiming it was hurting the economy

- They accused governor Njoroge of coming up with regulations and implementing them without their nod

- The MPs in 2018 passed the Finance Act which required the CBK boss to publish regulations governing banking sector

- They alleged Njoroge was yet to present a single regulation before the House even after the enactment of the Act

- The lawmakers are plotting to block renewal of the governor's appointment after his term expires in March 2019

Members of the 12th Parliament have decided to go after the Central Bank of Kenya (CBK) boss, Patrick Njoroge, claiming the governor has been making and enforcing regulations without the approval of the National Assembly as expected.

The legislators were especially miffed by the stringent rules that Njoroge introduced to cap the amount of money one can withdraw or deposit in any given day without attracting any special attention from the bank and the regulator.

While debating on the Finance Act they passed in 2018 and which required CBK to publish regulations governing the banking sector within 30 days before any implementation could commence, the MPs alleged Njoroge had been coming up with rules and enforcing them without their nod.

According to the lawmakers, the CBK boss was yet to present or publish a single regulation before the House almost half a year since the Finance Act 2018 came into force.

They questioned why the rules on daily cash transactions were not brought to Parliament for debate and approval before implementation, arguing the said regulations were hurting the economy by reducing liquidity.

"Rules are not supposed to make it difficult for people to do genuine business. But he (Njoroge) has made it more difficult for people to do business by turning banks into documentation bureaus,” Majority Leader Aden Duale protested.

Minority Whip Junet Mahamed added the contentious regulations capping daily withdrawals and deposits were illegal.

The Suna East MP called on the members to block renewal of Njoroge's term which is set to expire in March 2019.

The Central Bank in January 2016 introduced new rules on how banks should deal with transactions involving more than KSh 1 million.

According to the regulations, lenders were expected to know the source and recipient of customer deposits or withdraws of over KSh 1 million.

TUKO.co.ke understands the directive was aimed at increasing traceability of bank transactions and discouraging money laundering and terrorism financing.
