Netizens Disappointed in Millicent Omanga after Chips Vendor Claims She only Received KSh 350, Umbre

 Posted on June 22, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  506 words  |  Almeda Bohannan
  • Bevalyne Kwamboka and Senator Millicent Omanga have been washing their dirty linen in public for the better part of the day
  • It all started when Omanga posted on Twitter that her team gave the young entrepreneur cash, potatoes and an umbrella to help her grow her business
  • Kwamboka however clarified and claimed she only received potatoes worth KSh 350 and that the team took their umbrellas after taking photos
  • KOT called out the leader for going against his world and using PR gimmick to gain political mileage at the expense of the young lady

Kenyans on social media have called out Senator Millicent Omanga after claiming that she had given viral chip's vendor Bevalyne Kwamboka some cash and branded umbrellas to help her in her business.

The Tanga Tanga Nairobi winger had announced her benevolence to the world after her team hooked up with the roadside chips vendor, a young woman juggling between school and business.

However, things took a different turn when the 25-year-old claimed on Twitter that the honourable leader's team only bought her potatoes worth Ksh 350 and they took back their umbrella after the photo session.

"For clarification, team @MillicentOmanga only bought potatoes worth KSh 350 that came out of the vehicle with five umbrellas, took photos returned them and gave out one and two aprons. Hiyo mambo ya cash haikua plus stock ni ya 350. I bought the big sack and started the day," she said.

Kenyans on Twitter react

Their exchange got netizens chipping in to share their opinion on the matter.

Here are some of their reactions:

@NahashinKimemia said:

"This Millicent Omanga story is sad. She lied to her fellow Kisii, she lied to her fellow woman and she has lied to the youth that UDA is trying to appeal to as the 2022 race heats up. Millicent's team isn't spoiling her name only. They are portraying the hustler nation in poor light."

Omanga responded to the claims and said when she saw Kwamboka needed help to get an umbrella, she was out of the country and sent her team to dispatch it.

She, however, apologised for not meeting the 'high' demands the young entrepreneur expected from her and wished her all the best in her business.
