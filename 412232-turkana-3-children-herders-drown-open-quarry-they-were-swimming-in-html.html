A family in Turkana is mourning the death of three children who drowned in an open quarry at Kampi Mpya village.

The trio aged 11, eight and six years was looking after their family herd when they decided to take a break and swim in the dirty waters of an open water point at Kasarani.

Eyewitnesses of the Sunday, May 16, incident said they had warned the deceased of diving in the pool.

The quarry was reportedly sunk in 2011 by the Internally Displaced Persons (IDPs) who had set a camp in the area.

Consequently, the area residents called on the government to barricade the quarry to avert future accident.

The remains of the young boys were taken to the Lodwar Referal Hospital, awaiting a postmortem.

In a separate story, friends and relatives on Saturday, May 8, gathered in Karatina village, Nyeri county, for the burial of three kids who were burnt to death in their Nakuru house.

Leah Njoki, Teresa Wanjiru and Mildred Akal were the loving daughters of Nakuru police officer Mary Migwi who is attached to Miti Mingi Police Post in Gilgil but resides in Nakuru Town.

The officer was on duty at the time of the accident at her house, which she had just moved into three days before the incident.

The three, aged between 11 and two years, were buried next to one another in a ceremony conducted in strict observance of the Ministry of Health COVID-19 protocols.

The family house help also died in the Saturday, May 1, inferno.
