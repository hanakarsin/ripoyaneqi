Musician Damilola Praiseworth has expressed concern that to a large extent, society has made it look like it is a favour when a man helps with things like changing diapers, taking kids to school, or feeding his baby.

According to him, society is always quick to assume incompetence once they see a man involved in taking care of a child, thereby perceive the wife as being spoilt or controlling.

On his Twitter page, he remembered several incidences where he took front seat parenting and people who saw him seemed to wonder why he was doing so.

"I went to the pharmacy to pick some meds for our baby and the middle-aged female pharmacist on duty goes "please I want to give this to the mother instead". I told her I was the father and she says "I know, but you will be very busy so let me give the mother please," he lamented.

Apparently, the experiences do not just start and end with him as his friend too has been a victim of the misconstrued societal belief.

He reiterates that as much as there are irresponsible men, there exist sincere ones who want to do better and be responsible fathers, but they get pushed back by society with statements like "don't you know you are a man? Why are you doing this?"

"If it took two to bring a child into the world, why is one person automatically excused for being absent and not involved?" he questioned.

He adds that taking care of children should not be viewed as men are doing a favor, seeking praises, or assisting the woman.

That it should be done with all sense of responsibility, full ownership, service, love, and sacrifice.

"There's nothing 'macho' about being absent, and the earlier society realizes this, the better," he insisted.

In his closing sentiments, Damilola expresses that the true strength of a man lies in his vulnerability, empathy, service, love, and sacrifice without any sense of entitlement.

According to him, if we communicate this better, our generation will be better from it.
