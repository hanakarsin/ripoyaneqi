A family in Vihiga county has yet to bury their kin who died on February 4, 2020, after delivering her fourth born child in Migori county, where she had been living with her husband.

Lorrine Joy Lumadi's body has been lying at the Rosewood Nursing Home for over a year after a brawl between her mother Ruth Mugesani and her husband, Davis Were, on who should bury her body ensued.

Speaking to TUKO.co.ke, Lorrine's mother said Were could not bury the deceased because the couple, who she says had separated due to infidelity claims, had been in an informal marriage, and he had not paid her dowry yet.

Court orders

Several months of disagreements followed, with Were claiming he could not pay the dowry, yet that of his mother-in-law, a single mother, had not been paid.

According to court papers seen by TUKO.co.ke, he was then ordered to perform a Maragoli tradition and pay the dowry for him to bury the body.

However, several months passed, and Were did not perform the customary requirement that saw the court rule in January 2021 favouring Lorrine's mother, allowing her to bury the body.

He was further asked by the court that ordered the body be preserved at the hospital's morgue to take care of all the bills that had accrued over the months, totalling KSh 207,900, but he only paid KSh 70,000.

Lorrine's mother, who is a P1 teacher, tried to raise the remaining funds through well-wishers, but she could only raise KSh 50,000, leaving a balance of KSh 87,900.

Determined to give her daughter a decent send-off, she would spend most of her time following up on the cases, which saw her interdicted, and her other two children abandoned her because all her attention was on her late daughter.

However, that did not deter her efforts to bury her daughter.

After being chased from the Vihiga county governor's office, a meeting with the Migori governor Okoth Obado saw the remaining balance waived, and she was finally allowed to bury her daughter.

Decomposed body

However, on the day she went to collect her daughter's body from the funeral home for the burial ceremony, she was shocked to learn that the body she was given had decomposed beyond recognition.

According to her, the hospital had shut down its fridge, arguing it was uneconomical to have the fridge running with only one body.

"I am aggrieved that this body is damaged beyond identification. Reliable sources told me that the hospital owner, Roselyne Weche, ordered mortuary attendants not to let the morgue fridge run throughout since there was only one body," she narrated."So they would switch off the machine, and that is why the body was neglected and is completely damaged," added the aggrieved mother.

Mugesani's complaints were echoed by Were, who said the hospital argued it was uneconomical after all bodies were collected from the morgue when the government ordered all bodies to be buried within 72 hours after death.

However, the hospital has refuted the claims with a representative of the health facility, Shem Tindi, telling TUKO.co.ke the facility only suffered occasional blackouts and had at no point switched off the fridge.

Frustrated efforts

The tussle between Lorrine's mother and her estranged husband has seen her remains stay at the morgue for over one year, with her mother claiming Were has been frustrating her efforts to bury the body so he could eventually bury it against the court's ruling.

"When I went for the body, I was mishandled. I had taken the coffin and the clothing and promised to come for the body after three days," Mugesani narrated.

"Later on, going for the body with other people with a vehicle, we demanded to view the body first but they refused, packed the body in the coffin and asked us to view it at home. But I refused because I could not carry a body home not knowing which body I was carrying," she added.

According to her, the man has been frustrating her and even denied her access to her grandchildren, who live with their father after the demise of their mother.

Lorrine's mother says she has run out of money as all her savings have been spent in court cases, and her only source of income was terminated, while neighbours seem to have given up on her.

She is now appealing to well-wishers to help her raise funds to do a DNA test to determine her daughter's body and give her a decent send-off, or it will be interred at the Migori public cemetery.
