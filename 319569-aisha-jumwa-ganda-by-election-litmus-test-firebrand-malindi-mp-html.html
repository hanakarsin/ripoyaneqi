Aisha Jumwa: Ganda by-election litmus test for firebrand Malindi MP

 Posted on May 17, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  485 words  |  Hettie Henneman

- The by-election which is slated for October 17 attracted four aspirants

- The exercise is seen as a political contest between Raila and Ruto

- The DP's point person in the coast, Aisha Jumwa, has vowed to leave nothing to chance

- Her renewed vigour is undoubtedly a strategy to wrestle the region from Raila's firm grip

The battle to control coastal region politics ahead of 2022 is already taking shape in Malindi constituency with the area MP Aisha Jumwa vowing to teach ODM party a political lesson.

With a by-election in Ganda Ward slated for Thursday, October 17, the exercise is slowly turning to be a political contest between Deputy President William Ruto and ODM leader Raila Odinga.

Though Raila and Ruto have not made major pronouncements or appearances in the little known Ganda Ward, their troops are alive to the fact that the outcome will have a huge ramification going forward.

In deed it is a litmus test for the firebrand Malindi lawmaker who is looking to stem her authority far and beyond Kilifi county.

The mini poll attracted four aspirants, ODM's Reuben Katana, David Mitsanze of Kadu Asili, Joseph Ngumbao Kiponda of Jubilee Party and independent candidate Abdulrahman Omar whose 2017 election victory was invalidated by a court.

Aisha, who fell out with the Orange party and joined ranks with Ruto, is rallying behind the incumbent Omar and has promised to show Raila dust for attempting to kick her out of ODM.

Many political observers see her renewed vigour as a strategy to lay the ground for her Kilifi gubernatorial ambition and wrestle the region from opposition politics, being Ruto's coast point person.

On Wednesday, October 16, police arrested the legislator after she stormed the home of Katana where ODM agents reportedly held last minute discussions on the night of Tuesday, October 15.

Katana's uncle was shot dead during the melee that ensued.

Aisha does not shy away from controversies and has waded every storm that came her way with confidence and boldness, in fact, her colleagues have likened her to Giriama freedom fighter, Mekatilili wa Menza.

But for Ainabkoi MP William Chepkut, the arrest may portend a political doom for her otherwise flourishing career.

"Arresting politicians has always been a glory point of their career. But for Aisha Jumwa, this arrest following the death of an innocent boy might mark the end of her otherwise shining career," he claimed.
