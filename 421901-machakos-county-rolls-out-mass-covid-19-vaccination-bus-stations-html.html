The fight against the dreaded coronavirus has received a major boost after the county government of Machakos, through the department of health and emergency services, embarked on undertaking a mass COVID-19 vaccination drive at bus terminals.

According to the regional government, the health drive will take place from Monday, August 9, to Friday, August 13.

Identity of the bus stations

The exercise, targeted at PSV drivers, matatu touts, and Boda Boda riders, is being carried out at the following bus stations, Mlolongo, Athi River, Makutano, Machakos town, Matuu, Tala, Kangundo and Masii.

All chairpersons in matatu saccos and the boda boda riders association are urged to sensitise persons under them for vaccination through a circular.

This comes as the Ministry of Health, in conjunction with Nairobi Metropolitan Services last week, began a vaccination drive in residential areas within the capital city to upscale the fight against the COVID-19 pandemic.

Nairobi Metropolitan Services Community (NMS) Strategy Coordinator Julia Kimutai said they started with Embakasi and intended on reaching out to 20,000 people during the past weekend.

Community-centred approach

Speaking separately, National Multi-Agency Command Centre on COVID-19 representative Douglas Nasio said the drive is a community-centred approach to ensure members of the general public access the jab.

A similar vaccination drive was conducted in five other areas within Embakasi, namely Jacaranda grounds, Moto Moto SDA church, Utawala SDA church, Kariobangi market and Terminus SDA church Dandora Phase 3.

Two charged in court over COVID-19 protocols

On Tuesday, August 10, TUKO.co.ke reported that a magistrate's court in Nairobi freed two city residents after they were charged with failing to keep the one-metre social distance rule to curb COVID-19 spread during a meeting at Twiga Towers.

Chief magistrate Francis Andayi ruled the government had allowed public service vehicles to carry passengers to full capacity and as such, maintaining social distancing was difficult.

"I have considered the mitigation by the suspects and the fact that the government has eased some of the COVID-19 measures. Even if they were to keep the one metre social distance at the meeting, they won't achieve the same while using matatus," Justice Andayi stated.

The defendants, Kevin Onyango Ndungu, Joseph Olare Omondi and Christopher Muchai, were arraigned in court and charged with failing to maintain a social distance of not less than one metre from one another in a public place.

They were accused of being found close to each other on August 9, 2021, at Twiga Towers in Nairobi, hence flouting the social distancing rule.

They faced a total of three counts ranging from resisting arrest and assaulting a police officer contrary to the law.

Ndungu and Omondi pleaded guilty to not keeping social distance, and the court discharged them.

Christopher, however, denied the charge. On the other two counts of resisting arrest and assaulting a police officer, they all denied the charges and were released on a cash bail of KSh 10,000.
