Jubilee Asks Registrar of Political Parties to End Pact with PDR after It Rebranded to UDA

 Posted on July 31, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  452 words  |  Chauncey Koziol

- Jubilee Party signed a pre-election agreement with the Party of Development and Reforms (PDR) in the build-up to the 2017 General Election

- The PDR later rebranded to the United Democratic Alliance following the latter's successful registration in December 2020

- Jubilee Party Secretary-General Raphael Tuju said the relationship between the two parties had become untenable

The ruling Jubilee Party has expressed its unwillingness to complete the presidential journey with its coalition partner Party of Development and Reforms (PDR).

In a letter to the Registrar of Political Parties, secretary-general Raphael Tuju said the relationship between the two parties had become untenable hence Jubilee's intention to pull out of the pact and chart its own course.

"The Jubilee Party National Management Committee after considering the prevailing political and legal hurdles facing the proposed coalition, have found it improper and untenable to complete the formulation of the coalition," Tuju said.

The ruling party noted all the PDR officials engaged in the coalition talks had been changed and replaced by hostile ones who went ahead to field candidates outside the original areas covered the cooperation.

Apart from competing against its partner in the recent by-elections, the Jubilee accused PDR members of being disrespectful and continuously referring to it in derogatory terms.

The PDR rebranded to the United Democratic Alliance (UDA), leading to a lack of harmonisation. Tuju singled out the use of the hustler tag as one that goes against its Tuko Pamoja slogan.

"This is to request that you take note of the content of this letter and to put on record our revocation of the provisional coalition agreement lodged in your offices on May 6, 2018 that was never completed," Tuju wrote.

Allies of Deputy President William Ruto registered the newly-formed UDA, a development section of political observers said marked the end of the Jubilee Party.

Elgeyo Marakwet senator Kipchumba Murkomen, a trusted member of the Ruto team, had in January 2020 hinted at a mass exodus from Jubilee to UDA.

"As members of Jubilee, we are ready to move to another room known as UDA, and I'm asking the chairperson to provide us with the bedding. We are telling the eight million Jubilee supporters that in your father's house there are many rooms one being UDA," he said.
