1. 2001: Halo - Combat Evolved

This Xbox console game first came out on November 15th, 2001. Developers later released its Mac OS X and Microsoft Windows compatible versions. The game was a massive success in terms of sales against competitor video games.

Combat Evolved significantly boosted Microsoft and introduced a new standard for other FPS game developers. A decade later, this game was honoured with a remastered graphics universally version release. The split-screen functionality that allowed a couple of rivals to play concurrently was also a trendsetter in the world of video games.

2. 2004: Halo 2

Halo 2 was released in Xbox on 9th November 2004, about three years after the release of Combat Evolved. Xbox One and Microsoft Windows versions followed, making the franchise a competitive play Master Chief Collection. Halo 2 registered a huge success and even broke the initial sales record on the first day of release.

3. 2007: Halo 3

Microsoft Windows strategically released Halo 3 on 25th September 2007 to influence customers into purchasing the Xbox 360. The console was second in the line of the Master Chief Collection.

Both Windows and Xbox One releases followed speedily. Emphasis on the multiplayer feature saw fans finishing the story campaign much faster than in previous versions.

With this release came new maps and a trilogy arc for single-mode players. Even better, Halo 3 sales went a record higher, exceeding that recorded in Halo 2.

Halo 3 set the bar much higher with a single-day sale of $170 million. A week later, the figure increased to $300 million and eventually qualified the game as the highest-grossing release for the Xbox 360 console.

4. 2009: Halo Wars

This game-changing release came out a couple of years after Halo 3, on 26th February 2009. The events here take place 20 years before Halo: Combat Evolved, during the first encounters with the Covenant.

The game was initially released on Xbox 360, changing the play mode from a first-person shooter into a real-time strategy. Developers later launched Halo Wars on Xbox One and later Microsoft Windows.

5. 2009: Halo 3: ODST

The franchise installment came out concurrently with Halo Wars in 2009, but this one was released on 22nd September. The release swapped back to the FPS roots without focusing on the Master Chief character. Instead, it followed five ODSTs “Orbital Drop Shock Troopers.”

This Master Chief Collection game came out on Xbox 360 before it rolled out for Windows and Xbox One consoles users. The game was much better, but its high cost left players unsatisfied. The Disc One release supported a single-player mode while Disc Two was a variation of Halo 3 multiplayer mode.

6. 2010: Halo - Reach

The release on September 14th was the last in the series of Bungie developed Halo games. The ending of a contract with Microsoft saw Bungie moving to the Destiny Franchise. Reach appealed to many franchise fans for its multiplayer functionality, which guaranteed quality.

Reach became a trademark during the release anniversary for Halo I. Staunch fans believe this was the last release of an actual Halo game as the contract between Microsoft and Bungie ended.

7. 2011: Halo - Combat Evolved Anniversary

After a decade, a high definition remake of Halo: Combat Evolved was released. It is found on Xbox 360, Xbox One, and Microsoft Windows Master Chief Collection.

8. 2012: Halo 4

343 Industries started by installing Halo 4 on Xbox 360, and after that, Microsoft Windows and Xbox One. Developers created a new story on this game that was released on 6th November, leaving fans hanging and unable to relate with previous releases. Some fans, however, loved the play that came with Halo 4 under the Master Chief Collection.

9. 2013: Halo - Spartan Assault

8th July 2013 saw the release of this game found on Microsoft Windows, Microsoft Windows Phone, Xbox 360, Xbox One, and iOS. This mobile twin-stick shooter game was originally exclusive to Windows 8 phones, tablets, and PCs. Before the announcement, Spartan Assault was code-named Halo: Bootcamp.

10.2014: Halo - The Master Chief Collection

On 11th November, 343 released this combination of remastered first-person shooter video games. It is made up of Halo 2, Halo 3, Halo 3 ODST, Halo: Reach, Halo 4, and Halo: Combat Evolved Anniversary, together with their multiplayer modes.

Additionally, this version found on Xbox One and Microsoft Windows ships with access to the Halo 5: Guardians multiplayer beta.

11. 2015: Halo - Spartan Strike

12. 2015: Halo 5 - Guardians

Three years after Halo 4, on October 27th, came Halo 5 with a single-player campaign as a critical improvement feature. The feature struck the market, but some fans felt it did not meet the initial level of quality that came with Bungie installations.

Scrapping the split-screen feature was a downgrade, especially for fans who had been playing with friends for years. Even so, developers introduced several other hooking features such as the new multiplayer mode "Warzone."

13. 2017: Halo Wars 2

This RTS Halo game release on 21st February picked on the story from initial Microsoft Windows and Xbox One consoles. The game version ended the solo campaign and welcomed the RTS storyline into Halo 4 and Halo 5 trilogy.

14. 2018: Halo - Fireteam Raven

Found on Arcade cabinet platform, this Halo game was released on 10th July. At first, it was released as an exclusive to Dave & Buster's in North America. However, it was made international later on. The game is set in the timeframe of Halo: Combat Evolved.
