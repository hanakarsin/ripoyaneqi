A COVID-19 patient admitted at the Mbagathi Hospital has succumbed to the deadly respiratory disease barely hours after making a passionate appeal for oxygen.

On Thursday, July 29, Fiona Juma revealed her oxygen saturation level was at 52 and that she could not walk nor talk due to the low oxygen level.

In the heartwrenching appeal posted on her Facebook page, Juma took the opportunity to thank everyone who had been by her side ever since she contracted the disease.

"If this is my last post, then God, I want to say thank you to everyone who has been there for me ever since I contracted COVID-19," she captioned a photo of herself in a ventilator."I have been to Mbagathi Hospital, where I have been trying to get oxygen for hours and days. Although, I can barely walk nor speak, they are telling me there's no oxygen completely," she added.

According to the desperate woman, she had seen not just one but many people die due to lack of oxygen, further pleading with anyone having the precios comodity to help her.

A Facebook post by the champion of online fundraising, Ndungu Nyoro, announced that Juma had succumbed to the disease after she failed to secure oxygen.

Nyoro noted that Juma had been declared an Intensive Care Unit (ICU) patient, but the health facility could not admit her there because it was full.

"By evening she was an ICU candidate. Sadly, the hospital did not have a way of getting her into the ICU. There was no available space everywhere," wrote Nyoro.

"Eventually she lost the battle. Heartbreaking. This COVID-19 is messing," he added.

Juma's death came in the wake of COVID-19 cases escalating in the country with the Ministry of Health caution health facilities within the country do not have beds for patients.

In an update on COVID-19 containment measures Health Cabinet Secretary Mutahi Kagwe on Friday stated infections from the more virulent delta form have spread across the country.

Ban on Public gatherings

During the briefing, Kagwe announced a 30-day ban on all public gatherings, including office meetings noting events, especially political ones had emerged as superspreaders.

“When you call a political gathering in a street where people are many is it super spreading. There is no point in carrying the rallies, then we have more deaths then we go to the funerals and then cause more deaths,” Kagwe said.

While places of worship have been allowed to operate, the Ministry of Health urged the relevant parties to ensure compliance with MoH guidelines.
