Kenyan Woman and Iranian Lover Who Disappeared at JKIA Released by Police, Family Confirms

 Posted on June 22, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  406 words  |  Hettie Henneman
  • Stella Waithera and her Iranian partner, Ahmed Golabi, were on their way to Iran after a family visit
  • They vanished into thin air last week after being dropped at JKIA
  • The two were arrested on suspicion of terrorism but were set free on Thursday, September 30

A Kenyan couple who vanished into thin air last week at the Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (JKIA) has been released by the police.

The duo, Stella Waithera and her Iranian partner, Ahmed Golabi, were on their way to Iran after a family visit.

According to Daily Nation, the two had been arrested on suspicion of terrorism.

A family member revealed that Waithera and Golabi were set free on Thursday, September 30, by the Anti-Terror Police Unit (ATPU) officers.

"They were released and they boarded their plane to Iran via Instabul, Turkey," the source said.

Njunu's family was expecting to hear from Waithera calling to confirm that she and her husband had landed safely in Iran as it was their habit.

Mobile phones switched off

Their mobile phones were switched off, raising concerns.

"If there is any crime they might have committed, they have the right to be taken to court and be charged accordingly. But we believe that is not the case,'' said Njunu.

The authorities told the family that the couple never boarded their flight but were reluctant to produce the CCTV footage saying the system was being serviced.

''People being held incommunicado for a long time it gets so disturbing. My heart is broken,'' said the sobbing Njunu.
