Millie Odhiambo: I am not ashamed of being called greedy

 Posted on March 10, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  457 words  |  Enda Jantzen

- Mbita MP Millie Odhiambo has been vocal on rejecting salary pay cut as proposed by the Salaries and Remuneration Commission (SRC)

- She famously said the SRC's recommendation of slashing MP's salaries would turn her into a beggar

- The outspoken MP has once again said she is comfortable being called greedy over her remarks on the pay cut

The debate on MPs’ salaries pay cut has resurfaced after a few days’ lull with a legislator now demanding for the disbandment of the salaries commission.

Vocal Mbita MP Millie Odhiambo says the Salaries and Remuneration Commission(SRC) has done its work and should now pack and go home.

The MP during an interview on NTV on Wednesday, August 30 said she was not afraid of being called greedy for demanding for better pay.

The SRC has slashed MPs’ salaries from Ksh 710,000 to KSh 620,250 and also denied them a number of allowances.

But Odhiambo said she spends at least KSh 200,000 per week in assisting her constituents take care of funeral expenses among other needs.

This she added was one of the reason why MPs’ pay should not be reduced even by a single cent.

“I am happy to be greedy as long as my greed helps the people of Mbita,” she said adding that unless the government supports Kenyans to enable them take care of every day needs, she would not relent in asking for better pay.

The legislator has joined two other female MPs; Gladys Wanga(Homa Bay County) and Gathoni Muchomba(Kiambu) who have condemned the SRC for slashing their pay.

But Gathoni was forced to apologise after she was lambasted by her constituents who event threatened to recall her.

Odhiambo said the SRC top officials were earning more than her and that the commission should now be disbanded.

“Sarah Serem(SRC chairperson) earns twice as much as I do..they have finished their job of deciding how much civil servants should earn..they should go home,” said the Mbita legislator.

President Uhuru Kenyatta has vowed not to sign any bill meant to increase MPs’ salaries.

NASA leader Raila Odinga has also distanced himself from MPs in his coalition who are clamouring for more pay.
