City lawyer Donald Kipkorir has shared his personal experience with COVID-19.

Kipkorir contracted the virus in November 2020 and was immediately put on treatment and a three-month post-recovery journey that drained him financially.

The flamboyant advocate recalled moments when he gasped for air after his lungs collapsed, and doctors recommended he be put on oxygen.

"My lungs collapsed. I was gasping for air. I was on oxygen. I lost my voice. I was treated through IV. I was in solitary isolation for nearly a month. When I removed the oxygen to go to the bathroom that was hardly a meter away, it was like was drowning," he narrated.

Treating and managing the disease has so far cost him KSh 4 million, but he is yet to recover fully, although he is COVID-19 negative.

"Though I am negative, I not yet fully recovered. I suffer from what is called long-COVID. It has cost me nearly KSh 4 million to reach where I am now and not yet done. And there is no insurance for COVID-19," he added.

Despite having spent so much to beat the infection, Kipkorir advised people to embrace vaccination, whether government-procured or any other that will be available.

"There is no cost not worth paying to avoid COVID-19. It is the worst experience and very expensive to treat. It is therefore prudent for everyone to take the vaccines available; whether the free one, AstraZeneca from GoK or Sputnik V from the private sector," he advised.

Kipkorir and his colleague lawyer Ahmednasir Abdullahi received the Russian COVID-19 Sputnik V vaccine jab on Tuesday, March 30, despite the vaccine importation controversy.

"Today, I became the second Kenya to take the Russian COVID-19 Sputnik V vaccine. I have full confidence in the vaccine to protect me from all COVID-19 variants," Kipkorir said.
